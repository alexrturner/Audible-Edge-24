Description: <p>Set your alarm clock for a meditative sunrise listening session with Lily Tait.</p><p>In partnership with Tone List, WA Museum Boola Bardip presents an experimental concert series set against the backdrop of the sunrise, perched in the highest reaches of our eastern terrace.</p><p>We invite you to start your day early, sit with a coffee and take a moment to reflect in peace while watching Boorloo’s east metropolitan area light up from the base of the hills to the steps of the Museum, scored by Naarm-based violinst and composer Lily Tait.</p><p>The level 3 terrace nook (indoors, warm and undercover) transforms into a serene performance space for the artist to take you on a journey over one and a half hours of sunrise.</p><p>(If you couldn't get enough of Sun Returns with Matt Rösner, come back for more after the festival!)</p>

Accessibility: WA Museum Boola Bardip is wheelchair accessible, with multiple gender-neutral wheelchair accessible bathrooms located throughout the building. No alcohol will be served, but coffee will be available. The room is large and high-ceilinged, but no windows will be open to facilitate further ventilation. The room will be lit entirely by natural light from a large window behind the performers, besides a few dim lights to assist with visibility on walkways prior to the performance. There is no dedicated check-out space booked for this session, but comfortable seating is available throughout the museum around the corner from the performance.
